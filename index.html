Empowering physicians for over 30 years.

VistA stands for "Veterans Health Information Systems and Technology Architecture." Originally created in the 1980's, VistA is the Electronic Health Record actively developed and currently used by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), the largest healthcare system in the United States. VistA is a comprehensive system for managing large healthcare entities, providing functions ranging from prescription refills to employee timekeeping.

VistA is used by doctors around the world. Over 50% of the doctors in the In the U.S. have trained with VA, and countries such as Jordan have implemented it. In fact, a 2011 survey conducted by the American Academy of Family Physicians found VistA CPRS (VistA's current User Interface) in the top of 10 rankings of user satsifaction, and #1 in several categories.

OSEHRA + VistA = Rapid Innovation.

VistA is free and open-source. OSEHRA's version is licensed under the Apache 2.0 License

In 2011, VA established a non-profit organization to act as a 'custodial agent' for VistA, with the aim of collaboratively developing future VistA enhancements with the rest of the world. By creating this custodial agent, known as OSEHRA, releasing VistA to it, and redefining their their development practices around open-source, VA has made a strong commitment to build a community version of VistA which the industry may adopt, modify, and contribute to.

OSEHRA VistA is this community version of VistA. By taking a baseline instance of the code, creating univerally adoptable enhancements, and proliferating them to the community, VA can more easily share improvements, and adopt those made by the community at large.
