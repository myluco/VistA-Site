What is OSEHRA VistA?

VistA stands for "Veterans Health Information Systems and Technology Architecture." Having been in use since the 1980's, VistA is the Electronic Health Record actively developed and currently used by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), the largest healthcare system in the United States.

VistA is freeware. It has been successfully implemented and supported outside of VA at hospitals and clinics both within the U.S. and around the world, typically at a fraction of the cost of competing solutions.

VistA is also user friendly. A 2011 survey conducted by the American Academy of Family Physicians found VistA CPRS (VistA's current User Interface) in the top of 10 rankings of user satsifaction, and #1 in several categories.

In 2011, VA established a non-profit organization to act as a 'custodial agent' for VistA, with the aim of collaboratively developing future VistA enhancements with the rest of the world. By creating this custodial agent, known as OSEHRA, releasing VistA to it, and pushing to define open-source development practices, VA has made a strong commitment to build a universal copy of VistA which the community may adopt, modify, and contribute to.

OSEHRA VistA is the community version of VistA. By taking a baseline instance of the code, creating univerally adoptable enhancements, and proliferating them to the community, VA can more easily share improvements, and adopt those made by the community at large.
