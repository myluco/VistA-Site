Overview

VistA has been in development by the Department of Veterans Affairs for over 30 years, and has generations of knowledge integrated into its codebase. VistA is written in MUMPS (Mass blah blah blah), or M. This language is often referred to as "The Language of Healthcare." Many major Electronic Health Record Systems are written in MUMPS, including Epic, McKesson, blah blah blah.

For a technician to understand VistA, it is best to understand the system from a conceptual perspective, then delve into the technical details. This resources section first provides an overview of the history of MUMPS and VistA, and provides resources for interested parties to gain an understanding of the MUMPS language. It then progresses into an architectural overview of VistA, and how to interact with its most core components. Lastly, it points to supporting resources to enable individuals to explore the vibrant ecosystem which lives to support this technololgy.

These resources are in now way meant to encompass the complete body of knowledge surrounding VistA's over three million lines of terse code. In the spirit of the open source efforts to maintain and grow VistA, this site is licensed under Apache 2.0 and maintained using Github. Feedback or comments are greatly appreciated using the integrated ideascale, or more directly, Github pull requests for updates to this body of knowledge are highly encouraged.
